FlexiTex: Enhancing Texture Generation
via Visual Guidance

Anonymous Submission

Abstract

Although recent texture generation methods achieve impressive results by using text-to-image networks, challenges such as over-smoothness and inconsistency, hinder the application of these methods in real-world applications. To address this issue, we propose FlexiTex, a flexible framework towards high-quality texture generation, supporting text and image conditioning both. The core of our work is the Visual Guidance Enhancement module, which incorporates specific information from visual guidance into denoising on multiple views. This approach prevents variance degradation and preserves high-frequency details during the synchronization of the shared texture. Furthermore, we introduce a Direction-Aware Adaptation module that automatically designs direction prompts based on different camera poses, mitigating the Janus problem and maintaining semantically global consistency. Through experiments on meshes from various sources, we demonstrate that our method outperforms baseline methods both quantitatively and qualitatively, showcasing the potential in advancing texture generation for real-world applications.

Framework

The framework of FlexiTex. Given a mesh with corrected orientation and text/image prompts, the Visual Guidance Enhancement module extracts visual features to provide informative guidance during the denoising steps. The Direction-Aware Adaptation module then injects direction prompts according to camera poses, employing diffusion models that are more geometrically sensitive. Through iterative texture warpping and rasterization, our method generates high-fidelity textures of both rich details and multi-view consistency.
